# Abstract {.unnumbered .unlisted}

Operating an isolated photovoltaic microgrid requires anticipating how much energy will be generated and consumed, estimating how long the storage can sustain demand, and detecting performance losses such as panel soiling in time. Conventional monitoring systems record what has already happened but do not anticipate it, so planning battery use and maintenance rely on the operator's experience. This work addresses that gap with a web-based digital twin for isolated photovoltaic microgrids, taking the microgrid of the Technological University of Havana "José Antonio Echeverría" (CUJAE) as a case study. The system monitors the installation in real time, forecasts generation and consumption, simulates storage autonomy, and diagnoses panel cleanliness, integrating four artificial-intelligence models on a web architecture built with open-source technologies. All site configuration resides in data rather than in code, allowing it to be applied to other microgrids without reprogramming.

In the experimental analysis, carried out with historical data for Havana and public datasets, solar generation forecasting reached a coefficient of determination of 0.90 and the panel-cleanliness classifier a precision of 84 %; the system's behaviour was verified through test cases and automated tests, and it was deployed configured for the CUJAE microgrid. The results show that the digital twin provides useful and sufficiently accurate support for the operation, analysis, and maintenance of isolated photovoltaic microgrids; its design, with all configuration held in data, makes it applicable to other installations through configuration, and empirical validation at sites with a different climate is left as future work.

**Keywords:** digital twin, isolated photovoltaic microgrid, solar forecasting, machine learning, energy autonomy.
